## The Immersion Structure (What actually happens)
The factories are the syllabus. You will visit and understand the full value chain (Fiber → Yarn → Fabric → Processing → Garment → Market).
- **The Source (Spinning Mills):** Understanding micronaire, staple length, and how yarn quality dictates final garment price.
- **The Structure (Weaving/Knitting/Powerloom units):** Seeing the physical limitations of speed vs. design complexity.
- **The Transformation (Processing/Dyeing/Printing houses):** The chemical reality. Water usage, effluent treatment, and color matching under pressure.
- **The Assembly (Garment factories):** Line balancing, bottleneck identification, and the human element of production.
- **The Business (Logistics & Costing):** How Incoterms, currency fluctuation, and port delays kill margins.

## The Classroom vs. The Commercial Reality
- **Fiber Properties:** Taught in theory, but reality shows properties change based on spinner's speed and humidity.
- **Sustainability:** A concept until you see the actual cost of Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD).
- **Design:** A vision that often fights against machine limitations and MOQs.
- **Production:** Taught as linear and logical, but in reality, it is chaotic, circular, and driven by crisis management.

## The TNTX Philosophy (Anti-Tourism)
There are no welcome drinks, no rehearsed presentations, and no sanitized walkways. Participants will walk floors where noise levels are high, temperatures are real, and deadlines are tight. If a machine breaks or a color lot fails, you will watch how the floor manager solves it. This is not exposure. This is independence.
